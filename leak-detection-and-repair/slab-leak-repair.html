What is a Slab Leak?

In Big Spring, TX, homeowners often encounter the complex issue of slab leaks, which occur when water pipes beneath your home's concrete foundation develop leaks. These hidden leaks can lead to severe structural damage if not addressed promptly.

Signs of a Slab Leak

Identifying the early signs of a slab leak is crucial to preventing further damage. Look out for:

  • Sudden spikes in water bills
  • Damp spots on floors
  • Warm areas on the floor
  • Low water pressure
  • The sound of running water with no visible source

Causes of Slab Leaks

Understanding the causes can help prevent future leaks. Common causes include:

  • Pipe corrosion due to soil chemistry.
  • Excessive pressure from foundation shifts.
  • Abrasions from natural vibrations or shifting soil.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include a sudden increase in your water bill, the sound of running water when none is in use, damp or warm spots on the floor, low water pressure, and the growth of mold or mildew.

A slab leak can be detected using various methods such as electronic listening equipment, video pipe inspection, and pressure testing to identify the leak's location within the slab.

Slab leaks can be caused by various factors, including poor construction, corrosion of pipes, shifting soil or foundation, pipe abrasion, and chemical reactions between the water and pipe materials.

Repair methods include pipe re-routing, pipelining, or direct access through the slab by breaking through the concrete to reach the damaged pipe.

Coverage for slab leak repairs varies depending on your homeowner's insurance policy. Often, repairs may be covered if the leak resulted from a sudden and accidental event, but gradual leaks due to wear and tear are generally not covered.
