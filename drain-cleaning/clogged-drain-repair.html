Frequently Asked Questions

Common causes include hair buildup, food waste, grease accumulation, soap residue, and foreign objects falling into the drain.

It is not recommended to use chemical drain cleaners as they can damage pipes over time. Instead, try natural methods like baking soda and vinegar or use a plunger.

You can prevent clogs by using drain covers to catch hair and food particles, avoiding pouring grease down the drain, and regularly cleaning drains with baking soda and vinegar.

Call a professional if home remedies and plungers do not clear the clog, if you notice recurring issues, or if multiple drains in your home are clogged simultaneously.

Effective tools include a plunger, a drain snake, and a drain auger. For simple clogs, a plunger often works well. For deeper clogs, a drain snake or auger may be needed.
