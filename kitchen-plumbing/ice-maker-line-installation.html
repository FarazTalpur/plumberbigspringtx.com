Frequently Asked Questions

You will need a drill, adjustable wrench, tubing cutter, and pliers. Additionally, Teflon tape and a water line installation kit, which typically includes the tubing and connectors, are required.

Make sure to turn off the main water supply before beginning installation. Use a T-connection to attach the water line to the existing water supply. Ensure all connections are tightened securely and use Teflon tape to prevent leaks.

It is recommended to use copper or braided stainless steel tubing for durability and reliability. These materials are resistant to kinking and can handle the water pressure well.

Once installed, turn the water supply back on and check for leaks at all connection points. Then, ensure the ice maker is switched on; it may take a few hours for the first batch of ice. Check that ice is being produced properly.

Check that the water supply valve is fully open and there are no kinks in the water line. Ensure the ice maker itself is turned on. If the problem persists, verify the water pressure is adequate and the line filter (if installed) is not blocked.
