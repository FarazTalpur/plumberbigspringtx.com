A thorough inspection of your sump pump can prevent costly repairs in the future. Our detailed inspection process includes:

Frequently Asked Questions

You should test your sump pump at least once every three months or before the rainy season to ensure it's functioning correctly.

Disconnect the pump from its power source, remove it from the sump pit, and clean its grate and impeller using a garden hose. It's also important to remove any debris from the pit itself.

Signs that a sump pump should be replaced include unusual noises, continuous running, failure to start, or if it's more than 10 years old.

Regular maintenance includes checking the power source, cleaning the pump and the pit, inspecting the float and its switch, and testing the check valve.

If your sump pump isn't working, check the power source, make sure the discharge line isn't clogged, and inspect the float switch. If problems persist, consider calling a professional for repair or replacement.
